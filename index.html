Abstract

A key issue still needs to be solved: how can we recover the finer texture information when we super-resolve at huge upscaling factors? Despite advances in accuracy and speed of single picture super-resolution utilizing faster and deeper convolutional neural networks, this issue is still at the forefront of the field.
The mean squared reconstruction error has been the main focus of past studies. Although the images reconstructed by the previous techniques have a high Peak-Signal-to-Noise-Ratio (PSNR), they still fail to retain the finer textural information. Here, we implement SRGAN, a generative adversarial network (GAN) for images' Super Resolution(SR) which was introduced first in the paper "Photo-Realistic Single Image Super-Resolution Using a Generative Adversarial Network" in 2017.
According to our understanding, it was the first framework capable of predicting natural pictures that are photorealistic for upscaling factor of 4x. They provided a novel loss function which they named 'perceptual loss' that combines an adversarial loss with a content loss to accomplish this. With the aid of a discriminator network that has been trained to distinguish between super-resolved pictures and photo-realistic images, the adversarial loss pushes the solution to the natural image manifold. In addition, rather than using similarity in pixel space, they applied a content loss inspired by perceptual similarity. On open benchmarks, their deep residual network can salvage photorealistic textures from severely downscaled photos. GANs use a game theoretic approach to learn how to create from the training distribution through a minimax two-player game.
In this project, we aim to tackle the major issue of recovering Photo-Realistic images using SRGAN.
